## 2. Indonesian Women Entrepreneurs Statistics

### Market Size & Representation
- **64.5%** of MSMEs in Indonesia managed by women
- **60%** of Indonesia's 52 million SME sectors run by women
- **43%** of all entrepreneurs in Indonesia are women

### Business Size Distribution
- **85%**: Micro and small businesses
- **13%**: Medium enterprises
- **2%**: Large businesses

### Income Levels
- **Household contribution**: Women's income grew from 33.5% (2010) to 37.26% (2020)
- **Monthly spending**: Most spend $101-200 USD
- **F&B businesses**: Majority generate < IDR 10 million monthly (< $628 USD)

### Age Distribution
- **33.55%**: Ages 30-35
- **23.23%**: Ages 26-29
- **20.65%**: Ages 20-25
- **Most common**: Late twenties to mid-thirties

### Education Levels
- **47.10%**: High school completion
- **29.68%**: Bachelor's degrees
- **7.74%**: Diploma/technical education
- **Rest**: Advanced degrees or primary education

### Workforce
- **75%** of women-owned enterprises operate with 0-4 employees
- Majority in informal sector (service and trade)
- Located in suburban and rural areas

### Economic Contribution
- **Small businesses**: IDR 443 trillion ($36.5 billion) to GDP
- **Medium businesses**: IDR 421 trillion ($34.6 billion) to GDP

## 3. Warung Business Financial Data

### Market Overview
- **Total warungs**: 3.6-5.5 million nationwide (3 million permanent)
- **Retail contribution**: 70% of total retail transactions in Indonesia
- **FMCG spending**: 70% flows through warungs
- **Grocery spending**: 85% flows through warungs
- **Market size**: USD $110 billion

### Daily Revenue (General Warung)
- **Average daily revenue**: IDR 500,000 (~$32 USD)
- **Monthly revenue**: IDR 15 million (~$970 USD)

### Coffee Shop (Warung Kopi) Specifics

#### Revenue Ranges
- **Average monthly income**: IDR 10.8 - 20.2 million ($700-1,300 USD)
- **Higher performing shops**: IDR 30.2 million/month ($1,950 USD)
- **Daily revenue**: IDR 250,000 - 1,000,000 per day ($16-65 USD)

#### Operating Costs
- **Monthly costs**: IDR 8.4 million average
- **Working capital**: IDR 8.4 million/month (inventory, supplies)
- **Initial setup cost**: Separate from operating capital

#### Profit Margins
- **Gross profit margin**: 30% (coffee and food sales)
- **Net profit margin**: 40% of gross revenue
- **Average monthly profit**: IDR 11.8 million (~$760 USD)

#### Pricing
- **Coffee price**: IDR 7,000 per glass (~$0.45 USD)
- **Typical products**: Coffee, snacks, light meals

#### Financial Metrics
- **Return on Investment (ROI)**: 26.89%
- **Revenue/Cost ratio (R/C)**: 1.26
- **Break-even**: Typically achieved within first year

### Supply Chain Challenges
- Smaller warungs buy from larger warungs at retail prices
- Limited access to FMCG agents reduces profit margins
- Digital platforms (Warung Pintar) increase owner income by **89.5%**

### Digital Platform Impact
- Owners using Warung Pintar earn **15% above regional minimum wage**
- Improved supply chain access
- Better inventory management

## 7. Borrower Risk Factors (Real-World Context)

### Positive Risk Indicators
- Consistent daily revenue (even if modest)
- Long-term business operation (3+ years)
- Multiple income sources
- Community trust and social capital
- Cooperative lending group participation

### Negative Risk Indicators
- Seasonal revenue fluctuations
- Limited supply chain access
- No financial records
- Irregular income patterns
- High debt-to-income ratio

### Financial Literacy Impact
- Education level correlates with financial management
- Digital platform adoption improves outcomes
- Training programs increase business success rates

## 9. Regional Variations

### Java vs Outer Islands
- **Java**: Higher population density, better infrastructure
- **Outer Islands**: 70%+ of Amartha loans, less competition, higher growth potential

### Urban vs Rural
- **Urban**: Higher costs, more competition, digital adoption
- **Rural**: Lower costs, community-based, traditional methods

### Business Density by Province
- **West Java**: Highest concentration of SMEs
- **East Java**: Strong warung culture
- **Sumatra**: Growing microfinance market
- **Sulawesi**: Emerging opportunities
